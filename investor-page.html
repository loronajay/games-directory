01 // THE VISION
Build the next generation of competitive arcade gaming.
Jay’s Retro Arcade is designed to modernize the competitive spirit of classic arcades by combining a browser-based arcade platform, a growing library of original arcade-style games, player identity systems, global leaderboards, tournaments, and future connected arcade hardware. The long-term goal is to build a scalable arcade network where digital play and physical arcade culture reinforce each other.

Arcades were the original competitive gaming platform. Jay’s Retro Arcade is built to bring that culture into the internet age.

FOUNDER THESIS
02 // THE OPPORTUNITY
A gap exists between modern online gaming and classic arcade competition.

ARCADES CREATED COMPETITIVE GAMING CULTURE

High scores, local recognition, repeated play, rivalry, and social competition were all central to classic arcade success.

MODERN ONLINE GAMES LOST ARCADE SIMPLICITY

Many modern games are deep, but they often lack instant accessibility, quick restart loops, and highly visible score-driven competition.

CONNECTED TECHNOLOGY CHANGES THE MODEL

Today, player profiles, cloud rankings, online tournaments, and connected cabinets can turn local arcade culture into a global network.

05 // WHY THIS CAN WIN
Jay’s Retro Arcade is not just a game. It is a scalable system.

PLATFORM-FIRST ARCHITECTURE

Instead of building one cabinet around one title, Jay’s Retro Arcade is designed as an Arcade OS that can support multiple games, player systems, and future hardware products.

COMPETITIVE GAME DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

The game library focuses on score chasing, survival, speedruns, and repeatable short-session gameplay, the same loops that historically made arcade games sticky and social.

SOFTWARE-NATIVE EXPANSION

Because the platform is software-first, new games and features can be added more efficiently than in traditional arcade hardware models.

COMMUNITY-BUILT ADVANTAGE

The founder brings nearly two decades of grassroots competitive gaming experience, giving the platform a real understanding of how rivalries, events, and player communities actually grow.

07 // GO-TO-MARKET
Grow community first, then scale hardware.

PHASE 1 // DIGITAL PLATFORM TRACTION

Grow the online arcade platform through weekly competition, leaderboard-driven engagement, and community building.

PHASE 2 // TOURNAMENT CULTURE

Develop recurring events, named challenges, and visible player identity systems that turn the platform into a real competitive destination.

PHASE 3 // PROTOTYPE CABINETS

Deploy early hardware prototypes for demonstrations, events, local tournaments, and proof of concept.

PHASE 4 // NETWORKED ARCADE ECOSYSTEM

Scale toward connected cabinets, broader competitive infrastructure, and a larger community spanning both online and physical spaces.

08 // FOUNDER
Built by someone who has already spent years building competitive gaming communities.
Jay Lorona has spent nearly twenty years participating in and organizing grassroots competitive gaming communities, especially through the Super Smash Bros. Melee scene. He hosted tournaments from his own apartment, helped grow local tournament culture in Sacramento, and later ran beginner-friendly competitive events that brought large numbers of new players into the scene. Over time, his apartment also became a miniature arcade hub where players gathered for retro game nights built around simple but deeply competitive games. Jay’s Retro Arcade is the direct evolution of that experience.

09 // LONG-TERM GOAL
A global community built around charm, competition, and quick-play arcade design.
In the long term, Jay’s Retro Arcade aims to become a recognizable home for original arcade-style games, competitive leaderboard chasing, game-specific subcommunities, recurring online and live tournaments, and future cabinets that extend the platform into the physical world. The project is designed not only as a business, but as a lasting ecosystem that can grow across generations.
